Anxiety a part of life, that can affect anyone.It is kind of like a natural response to stress or a "threat". It is the "gut feeling" we all get right before making a wrong/dangerous decision. But sometimes, when When anxiety becomes unbearable and remains, an anxiety disorder may be present.But if is managable with the help of nessary treatmeant and support form family and friends.

There are many types of Anxiety disorders, some they are-

  • Generalized anxiety disorder (GAD): Excessive worrying about day-to-day problems for six months or more.
  • Panic disorder: Getting frequent and unexpected panic attacks.
  • Social anxiety disorder: Significant anxiety, self-consciousness, and shame result from the worry of being noticed or negatively analysed by others in regular interactions.
  • Separation anxiety disorder: An anxiety disorder characterised by overwhelming fear and anxiety about being away from a loved one. It's among the most common anxiety disorders among children and teens

    • Even though Anxiety seems and feels scary, there are many treatments to help teens with this disorder. Some of them are-

  • Therapy: Talking to a counciler or professional can help teens a lot, like sorting out their feeling and can also be a way for them to vent out their thoughts and feelings
  • Medication: Healthcare professionals may prescribe anti-anxiety medications, such as selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) to help manage severe anxiety symptoms.
  • Relaxation Techniques: Teaching teens relaxation skills can help them deal with stress and anxiety more skillfully. Examples of these approaches include progressive muscle relaxation, deep breathing exercises, and mindfulness meditation. They can include these methods into their everyday routine to support emotional equilibrium and general well-being.
